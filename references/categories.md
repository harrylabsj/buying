# Categories

Use this file when the user asks about a product type, industry, or category-specific buying decision.

## Electronics And Tech

Focus on:
- exact model and spec matching
- warranty and official channel status
- bundle equivalence
- return policy
- whether the item is new, refurbished, open-box, or gray-market

Watch for:
- old model sold as current mainstream option
- unclear activation or region lock
- missing accessories
- reseller listings framed like official listings

## Vehicles

Focus on:
- recent sold comps
- mileage, condition, accident history
- title status
- maintenance records
- total cost after taxes, fees, repairs, insurance

Watch for:
- unusually low price without clean documentation
- seller reluctance to share VIN or records
- urgent-sale pressure

## Real Estate

Focus on:
- comparable sold properties
- neighborhood trend
- days on market
- financing rate context
- hidden repair or HOA costs

Watch for:
- staged urgency
- weak disclosure
- pricing that assumes unrealistic appreciation

## Services

Examples:
- contractors
- legal or accounting services
- internet providers
- insurance

Focus on:
- scope clarity
- total fees, not teaser price
- contract length
- cancellation terms
- competitive alternatives

Watch for:
- bait pricing
- auto-renew traps
- vague deliverables

## B2B And SaaS

Focus on:
- true annual cost
- seat minimums
- feature gating
- switching cost
- contract lock-in
- overlap with existing tools

Watch for:
- "discount" that only applies to year one
- weak termination clauses
- hidden implementation cost

## Groceries And Commodities

Focus on:
- per-unit normalization
- freshness and fulfillment quality
- threshold requirements
- shipping and cold-chain fees

Watch for:
- fake big discounts on smaller pack sizes
- frozen vs fresh mismatch
- low unit price erased by delivery fees

## Subscriptions

Focus on:
- actual usage
- duplicate tools
- cancellation difficulty
- cheaper annual or family plan alternatives

Watch for:
- silent price increases
- multiple overlapping subscriptions
- legacy plans that no longer make sense
